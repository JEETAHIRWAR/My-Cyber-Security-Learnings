Domain 1: Foundations of Cybersecurity

Overview

This chapter provides a comprehensive introduction to the fundamental principles and concepts of cybersecurity. It covers essential topics such as the CIA triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability), authentication methods, non-repudiation, privacy laws, risk management, compliance standards, security controls, and security awareness training.

Learning Objectives

After completing this domain, the participant will be able to:

  • Understand the principles of confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA) in the context of cybersecurity.
  • Explore various authentication methods including passwords, biometrics, and tokens.
  • Learn about non-repudiation and the importance of digital signatures.
  • Relate risk management to personal or professional practices.
  • Gain knowledge of privacy laws and regulations such as GDPR and HIPAA.
  • Learn about risk management techniques, compliance standards, and threat modeling.
  • Understand basic cryptography concepts, access controls, and security policies development.
  • Recognize the significance of security awareness training for employees.
  • Practice the terminology and review security principles.

Key Topics Include

  • Principles of CIA (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability)
  • Authentication Methods (Passwords, Biometrics, Tokens)
  • Non-repudiation and Digital Signatures
  • Privacy Laws and Regulations (GDPR, HIPAA)
  • Risk Management and Compliance
  • Security Controls
  • Basic Cryptography Concepts
  • Access Controls
  • Security Policies and Procedures Development
  • Security Awareness Training for Employees
