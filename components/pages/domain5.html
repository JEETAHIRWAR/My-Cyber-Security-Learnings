Domain 5: Incident Response and Forensics

Overview

Welcome to Domain 5: Incident Response and Forensics provides learners with essential knowledge and skills to effectively respond to cybersecurity incidents and conduct digital forensic investigations. From understanding incident response processes to implementing advanced threat hunting techniques, this domain covers critical aspects of incident response and digital forensics.

Learning Objectives

After completing this domain, the participant will be able to:

  • Understand the incident response process, including incident detection, analysis, containment, eradication, post-incident recovery, and lessons learned.
  • Learn digital forensics fundamentals, including digital evidence collection, preservation, forensic imaging, and chain of custody procedures.
  • Gain proficiency in analyzing volatile and non-volatile data to extract evidence and insights from digital devices.
  • Explore forensic analysis techniques such as file system forensics (NTFS, ext4), memory forensics using tools like Volatility, and network packet analysis with Wireshark.
  • Understand advanced threat hunting methodologies and their role in proactively identifying and mitigating cybersecurity threats.
  • Learn about threat intelligence integration and its significance in enhancing incident response capabilities.
  • Explore security orchestration, automation, and response (SOAR) platforms and their role in automating incident response processes.
  • Familiarize yourself with cyber threat hunting methodologies to proactively detect and respond to emerging threats.
